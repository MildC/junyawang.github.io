Street Artist Magazine Spread Design

Graphic Design (magazine)

Black and white magazine spreads design for a subject that involves images of human faces and figure, done individually.

The theme I choose was street artist, then I took photos of them by myself, and use these photos to design the spread.

My Role: Graphic Design

Tool used: Adobe Illustrator, Adobe Photoshop, Olympus E510

Designed in October 2012

Story Behind Project

This individual assignment was to degisn 3 black and white magazine spreads for any subject of students' choice that involves images of human faces and figure. Images used on magazine has to be students' original photography work, which requires students to consider about composition of a whole spread when shooting pictures. I chose street artists as my subject because they are a considered as a local customs and culture of downtown Vancouver.

I used Olympus Evolt E-510 to record several street artists during their working in downtown Vancouver and chose different artists or photos from different angles and did proper crops based on scale of faces to create variety and adjust images for underlying structure. During design process, focusing on underlying structure, I have also applied design grammars such as creating hot spot and high contrast, leaving white space and paying attention to the strong power of eye line, which leads readers where to look next.

This is my first time doing magazine design applying underlying structure and taking pictures of myself. I learned that when everytime taking a picture for magazine, designers should kind of having ideas of what would the spreads look like beforehand.
